Task 4:Draft a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide


Poor digital communication can sometimes lead to misunderstandings and real problems. For example, imagine a student sending a message to a professor through email without a clear subject line and using very informal language. The message may simply say, “Send me the assignment details,” without mentioning the course name or providing proper context. Because of this unclear communication, the professor might misunderstand the request or ignore the email, thinking it is spam or unprofessional. As a result, the student may miss important information about the assignment deadline.

This situation could have been avoided by following proper digital communication practices. The student should have written a clear subject line, used a polite greeting, explained the request properly, and included their name and course details. Using respectful and structured communication helps avoid confusion and ensures that messages are understood correctly.
