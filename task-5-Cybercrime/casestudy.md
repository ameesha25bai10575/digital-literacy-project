Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which criminals trick people into transferring money through online payment apps. As digital payments have become popular, fraudsters are using new methods to exploit users who are not aware of online security risks.

How the Crime Happens
-A typical UPI fraud begins when the victim receives a phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp message from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or online seller. The fraudster may claim that the victim has won a prize, needs to verify their account, or will receive a refund.
-First, the scammer sends a UPI payment request or a suspicious link to the victim.
-Second, the victim is asked to approve the request or enter their UPI PIN, believing that they will receive money.
-Third, once the victim enters the PIN or approves the request, the money is transferred from their bank account to the fraudster’s account instantly.

Who is Targeted
These scams often target students, elderly people, and new digital payment users who may not fully understand how UPI transactions work.

Consequences
The victim may lose a significant amount of money, and recovering the funds can be difficult. Victims may also experience stress and loss of trust in digital payment systems.

Conclusion
To prevent UPI fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid clicking unknown links, and verify payment requests carefully before approving any transaction.
