Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

Online Safety Tips

1.Use strong and unique passwords- for your email, social media, and academic accounts. Avoid simple passwords like your name or birthdate.
2.Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)- whenever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
3.Do not click on suspicious links- received through email, SMS, or social media, especially from unknown sources.
4.Be careful when sharing personal information online-, such as your phone number, address, or student ID details.
5.Always verify websites before entering login details- to avoid phishing scams. Check the website URL carefully.
6.Keep your devices updated- with the latest security updates and antivirus software.

 Financial / UPI Safety Tips

1.Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank or customer support.
2.Do not approve unknown UPI payment requests-; remember that entering your UPI PIN means you are sending money, not receiving it.
3.Use official banking or payment apps only, and avoid installing apps from unknown links.

Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime or financial fraud, immediately report it through:

1.National Cyber Crime Portal:[https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
2.Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930

Reporting quickly can help authorities take action and possibly recover lost funds.
