# digital-literacy-project
 This project explores key areas of digital literacy for students, including an infographic, professional digital portfolio, coding practice, email etiquette, and cybercrime awareness. It uses Canva, GitHub, Kaggle, Stack Overflow, HackerEarth, and Google Forms to demonstrate safe and responsible digital skills It promotes safe and ethical tech use.

Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
1.Tool Used:For this project, I used Canva to design my infographic. I selected Canva because it provides a variety of easy-to-use templates, icons, and design elements that help in creating visually attractive educational content. It allowed me to organize information clearly while also adding relevant icons and simple graphics. Using Canva made it easier to present important information in a way that is both engaging and easy to understand for students.
2.Design Overview:My infographic is organized into three main sections to explain the key aspects of digital literacy. The first section explains what Digital Literacy is, describing it as the ability to use digital technology effectively, responsibly, and safely. The second section focuses on Useful Digital Tools for Students, highlighting platforms such as Google Docs for collaboration, Canva for design, Miro for brainstorming, and online learning platforms like Coursera and Khan Academy. The third section emphasizes Safe Internet Practices, where I included tips such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information online.
3.Reflection:While working on this infographic, I realized that digital literacy is an essential skill for students in today’s technology-driven world. One challenge I faced was organizing the information so that it remained simple and visually clear without adding too much text. At the same time, choosing suitable icons and arranging the sections creatively was very interesting. This task helped me understand how visual design can make educational information easier to read and remember.

Task 3: Coding and Collaboration Platforms
1.Platforms Used:
For this task, I explored HackerEarth for coding practice and Google Workspace tools (Google Forms and Google Sheets) for digital collaboration. These platforms are very useful for computer science students because they help improve programming skills as well as data management and teamwork. HackerEarth provides coding challenges that strengthen logical thinking, while Google Workspace tools help in creating forms, collecting responses, and organizing data efficiently.
2.Project Overview:
In Part A, I used HackerEarth to solve a programming problem where I had to find the smallest prime number that divides two given integers. The task required analyzing both numbers and determining whether they share a common prime factor. If a common prime divisor exists, the program prints the smallest one; otherwise, it prints -1. For example, when the input values were 3 and 6, the output was 3, since 3 is the smallest prime number that divides both numbers. Solving this problem helped me practice logical thinking and better understand prime numbers and problem-solving using code.

In Part B, I created a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms. The quiz included five questions with different formats such as multiple-choice and short-answer questions. I then linked the form responses to Google Sheets, which automatically collects and organizes the responses in a spreadsheet for easy analysis.

Task 4:Draft a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
-Poor digital communication can sometimes lead to misunderstandings and real problems. For example, imagine a student sending a message to a professor through email without a clear subject line and using very informal language. The message may simply say, “Send me the assignment details,” without mentioning the course name or providing proper context. Because of this unclear communication, the professor might misunderstand the request or ignore the email, thinking it is spam or unprofessional. As a result, the student may miss important information about the assignment deadline.
This situation could have been avoided by following proper digital communication practices. The student should have written a clear subject line, used a polite greeting, explained the request properly, and included their name and course details. Using respectful and structured communication helps avoid confusion and ensures that messages are understood correctly.

Task 5:Cybercrime
-Project Report Reflection
While researching cybercrime and online safety, I was most surprised by how easily people can become victims of digital fraud, especially through simple tricks like phishing messages or fake UPI payment requests. Many cybercriminals take advantage of people who are not fully aware of how online scams work. I learned that even a small mistake, such as clicking on an unknown link or approving an unfamiliar payment request, can lead to serious financial loss or data theft.

This research helped me understand the importance of being careful while using digital platforms. One habit I will personally change is carefully verifying any online payment request or link before taking action. I will also avoid sharing personal or financial information online and use stronger passwords to protect my accounts. Learning about cybercrime has made me more aware of digital safety and responsible internet usage.
