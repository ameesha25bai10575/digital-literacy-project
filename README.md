# digital-literacy-project

It uses Canva, GitHub, Kaggle, Stack Overflow, HackerEarth, and Google Forms to demonstrate safe and responsible digital skills It promotes safe and ethical tech use.

Part B: Social Media Do's and Don'ts
Responsible Social Media Use & Digital Communication – Checklist for College Students
Do’s
-Think before posting or sending messages to ensure your communication is respectful and appropriate.
-Protect your privacy online by adjusting privacy settings and avoiding sharing personal information.
-Use social media and digital platforms for learning and collaboration with classmates and teachers.
-Maintain polite and professional digital communication in emails, comments, and messages.
-Verify information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation.
Don’ts
-Don’t share sensitive personal details such as passwords, address, or phone numbers online.
-Don’t post offensive, harmful, or inappropriate content that may damage your reputation.
-Don’t engage in cyberbullying or negative online arguments.
-Don’t forward fake news or unverified information.
-Don’t misuse digital communication platforms by sending spam messages or disrespectful comments.
Poor digital communication can sometimes lead to misunderstandings and real problems. For example, imagine a student sending a message to a professor through email without a clear subject line and using very informal language. The message may simply say, “Send me the assignment details,” without mentioning the course name or providing proper context. Because of this unclear communication, the professor might misunderstand the request or ignore the email, thinking it is spam or unprofessional. As a result, the student may miss important information about the assignment deadline.

This situation could have been avoided by following proper digital communication practices. The student should have written a clear subject line, used a polite greeting, explained the request properly, and included their name and course details. Using respectful and structured communication helps avoid confusion and ensures that messages are understood correctly.
Task 5:Cybercrime
Part A – Cybercrime Case Study
Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which criminals trick people into transferring money through online payment apps. As digital payments have become popular, fraudsters are
using new methods to exploit users who are not aware of online security risks.
How the Crime Happens
-A typical UPI fraud begins when the victim receives a phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp message from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or online seller. The fraudster may claim that the victim has won a prize, needs to verify their account, or will receive a refund.
-First, the scammer sends a UPI payment request or a suspicious link to the victim.
-Second, the victim is asked to approve the request or enter their UPI PIN, believing that they will receive money.
-Third, once the victim enters the PIN or approves the request, the money is transferred from their bank account to the fraudster’s account instantly.
Who is Targeted
These scams often target students, elderly people, and new digital payment users who may not fully understand how UPI transactions work.
Consequences
The victim may lose a significant amount of money, and recovering the funds can be difficult. Victims may also experience stress and loss of trust in digital payment systems.
Conclusion
To prevent UPI fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid clicking unknown links, and verify payment requests carefully before approving any transaction.
Part B:Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students
Online Safety Tips

1.Use strong and unique passwords- for your email, social media, and academic accounts. Avoid simple passwords like your name or birthdate.
2.Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)- whenever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
3.Do not click on suspicious links- received through email, SMS, or social media, especially from unknown sources.
4.Be careful when sharing personal information online-, such as your phone number, address, or student ID details.
5.Always verify websites before entering login details- to avoid phishing scams. Check the website URL carefully.
6.Keep your devices updated- with the latest security updates and antivirus software.

 Financial / UPI Safety Tips
1.Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank or customer support.
2.Do not approve unknown UPI payment requests-; remember that entering your UPI PIN means you are sending money, not receiving it.
3.Use official banking or payment apps only, and avoid installing apps from unknown links.

Reporting Cybercrime
If you become a victim of cybercrime or financial fraud, immediately report it through:
1.National Cyber Crime Portal:[https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
2.Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
Reporting quickly can help authorities take action and possibly recover lost funds.
